ABOUT US

Mission Statement

TrickFire is all about giving our members hands-on education that can only be experienced working on a large-scale project. For the last several years, this project has been the NASA Robotics Mining Competition, an annual event that pits fifty college robotics teams around the nation to build the most efficient autonomous lunar mining rover. The team works year-round to design, manufacture, and test the rover, just in time to travel to Kennedy Space Center, Florida for the competition.


Our team's mission can be narrowed down into three pillars:
1. Technical development by working on industry leading robotics
2. Professional development by working with a large scale team.
3. A strong community within the University of Washington Bothell.

Mechanical

The mechanical team focusses on the physical design and assembly of the robot. The team faces the challenge of designing a rover that can mine up to its height below the surface while keeping the weight to under 60kg. The team succeeds in producing an efficient rover by incorporating various light weight composites with elegant design.

Software

The software team focusses on rover functionality and rover autonomy. They have to work diligently to make sure that their code is properly written, or else it can spell disaster for the robot. From low level CAN bus programming to high level autonomous navigational algorithms, this team does a lot!

Electrical

The electrical team focus on circuitry for signal and power management. With every motor and sensor needing different voltage levels and power distribution, the team works hard to ensure all of them are efficiently integrated through circuit design and power control. With their help the transition between mechanical parts and the software control is seamless.

Finance

The finance team is the backbone of the backbone of TrickFire's technical side. Their goal is to secure funding for everything that is done in the team - to build a rover can cost tens of thousands of dollars. It is the job of this team to plan years in advance to ensure the coffers are always filled, either by applying for grants or finding industry sponsors.

Outreach

If a robotics team makes a rover, but no one knows about it, was the rover really built? Our outreach team puts TrickFire Robotics on the map! Being the backbone of TrickFire’s technical side includes:
•Recruiting new members
•Reaching out to local industry
•Handling public relations
•Keeping our social media up to date
